VoxeraOS North Star (plain text projection)
===========================================

This is the plain-text projection of docs/NORTH_STAR.md, kept here so the
text-only bundle is self-contained. The canonical version lives in
docs/NORTH_STAR.md.

The vision
----------

Build a voice-first AI operating system that feels alive: an AI you can
actually talk to, that can think with you, explore ideas, test
possibilities, and help you move fast. That feels less like a tool and
more like a real teammate.

That is Vera.

For Vera to matter in the real world it cannot just be clever. It has
to be trustworthy. That is what Voxera OS is for.

Vera is the intelligence. Voxera OS is the trust layer.

Vera reasons freely. Voxera OS controls execution.

Vera thinks wildly; Voxera acts carefully.
Vera explores; Voxera enforces.
Vera improvises; Voxera makes sure nothing important happens silently,
unsafely, or without proof.

The boundary
------------

If something can affect the real world -- a config change, a service
restart, a file mutation, a privileged action, an API call with side
effects -- it must go through Voxera OS first.

Voxera OS is the capability gateway. It is the control layer. It is the
audit spine.

All real-world side effects are capability-gated, policy-evaluated, and
evidence-backed.

How Vera proposes work
----------------------

When it is time to actually change something, Vera does not freestyle.
It submits a structured request through Voxera OS that says:

- what it wants to do
- what capability it needs
- what it plans to touch
- what outcome it expects
- how success will be verified

Voxera OS evaluates that request against policy, scope, and runtime
safety. The answer is always one of:

- Allow  -- safe and in scope
- Ask    -- needs human approval
- Deny   -- unsafe, out of scope, or not trusted

When the system is unsure it does not guess. It fails closed.

Why this matters
----------------

A lot of AI systems chase autonomy. We are chasing something more useful:
deployability. Not an AI that "can do anything," but an AI that can do
meaningful things safely, visibly, and under control.

That means:

- no silent side effects
- no hidden mutation paths
- no policy bypass through prompt tricks
- no unaudited "helpful" execution
- no direct real-world changes outside Voxera OS

If something important happened, there should be evidence. Real,
replayable, inspectable evidence -- not vague logs buried somewhere, not
"we think this is what the model did." A record of:

- what Vera requested
- what Voxera decided, and why
- what actually executed
- what changed
- what succeeded or failed
- what artifacts prove it

That is what makes the system operational. That is what makes it
trustworthy at 2 AM during an incident.

Non-negotiables
---------------

- No silent side effects
- No direct mutation paths outside Voxera OS
- No policy bypass through prompts or metadata drift
- No un-audited execution
- No privileged real-world changes without going through the gateway
- When uncertain, fail closed

What success looks like in v1
-----------------------------

- 100% of mutating actions go through the capability gateway
- 0 privileged mutations happen outside the gate
- 100% of executed jobs produce an inspectable evidence bundle
- Read-only and interactive flows remain fast
- Recovery and replay work cleanly after interruption, without
  duplicate side effects

What we are not building
------------------------

- just another chatbot
- a reckless shell-running agent
- opaque autonomy with weak traceability
- "AI magic" that becomes impossible to trust under pressure

Final thought
-------------

Keep the magic free. Keep execution controlled. Make everything important
become evidence.

That is Vera. That is Voxera OS. That is the North Star.
